HERITAGE SITE

Dambulla | Sigiriya

  • Location: northern Matale District near the town of Dambulla in the Central Province, Sri Lanka.
  • Highlights: 200 meter high rock
  • open hours: 6am-4pm
  • Things to Keep in Mind: take water with you before you enter the paid area

Sigiriya is well-known for its palace ruins, which are perched atop a towering 200-meter-high rock and are encircled by the remnants of a vast network of gardens, reservoirs, and other buildings.

Beginning in the fifth century BC, it served as a rock-shelter mountain monastery, with caves prepared and donated by followers of the Buddhist Sangha. The Sigiriya Museum exhibits images of the site's excavation, copies of the paintings, illustrations and translations of some of the graffiti on the Mirror Wall, and items found there. Dambulla | Dambulla CaveTemple

  • Location: Dambulla

The first century BCE is when this temple complex originally appeared. The most famous cave temple in Sri Lanka is this one. It includes five caves with drip lines carved into the walls to keep the inside dry. Various gods and goddesses, as well as colourful religious images, can be seen inside the cave ceilings. Both the Lord Buddha and bodhisattvas are depicted. The caves were constructed between the Anuradhapura and Polonnaruwa periods at the foot of a 150-meter-high cliff. Devaraja Lena, Maharaja Lena, Maha Alut Viharaya, Paccima Viharaya, and Devana Alut Viharaya are the names of the five caves.

Sacred City of Anuradhapura

  • Location : Anuradhpura

The earliest historic capital of Sri Lanka and one of the eight UNESCO World Heritage Sites is the sacred city of Anuradhapura. The city of Anuradhapura is home to a large network of historic temples and monasteries that span over 40 square kilometres.

It is the city with the greatest concentration of cultural landmarks. The majority of these Buddhist temples have survived for thousands of years with little damage. Buddhist pilgrims hold it in the highest regard as one of Sri Lanka's most sacred cities.
