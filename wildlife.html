WILD LIFE

Horton Plains National Park

  • Location: Ohiya, Sri Lanka
  • Highlights: Sunrise at World’s End
  • Best Time: November to March
  • Things to Keep in Mind: The trek is not very easy throughout the trail especially near the falls. Reach at World End before sunrise for best view

Horton Plains National Park, which was created in 1988, is one of the best locations in Sri Lanka for hiking. It acts as a showcase for the country's stunning landscape as well as its large and exclusive flora and wildlife. The ideal way to travel through the park is on foot because it has great trekking routes that span a distance of 9 kilometres, even though there is the option of taking a jeep ride.

Along with the pictorial scenic beauty one can also see Sri Lanka Samba deer, wild boar and spot macaques. At an Altitude-2100-2300 meter, World’s End serve as the end point of this hiking tour. The look out from this point is simply amazing and provides an expansive terrain.

1000 LKR

Wasgamuwa National Park

  • Location: Matale and Polonnaruwa Districts. 5 hours drive from Colombo.
  • Timing: 5.30am to 6.00pm
  • Additional Price: Tickets per adult is LKR 2,000 and per child is LKR 1,000.
  • Duration: Full Day safari duration is 8 hours.

One of the best sites in the world to view wildlife at its best is Sri Lanka. The island's Wasgomuwa National Park, with its thriving flora and animals, is located in the island's arid lowlands. It exhibits a wide diversity of life.

This 36,900-hectare national park is home to 23 different mammal species, as well as a variety of birds and plant species. It is well known for having numerous elephant families. While it will be challenging to see a leopard or sloth bear, it is simple to see the endemic purple-faced langur.

2000 LKR

Sinharaja Forest Reserve

  • Location : Sinharaja Forest Reserve, Southern Province
  • Timings: Opens daily from 6:30 AM to 6 PM
  • Price: Approximate price ranges from INR 260 to INR460 (664 LKR to 1160 LKR)

Sinharaja Forest Reserve, also known as the "Kingdom of the Lion," is a haven for those who enjoy the outdoors and wildlife. UNESCO has designated it a World Heritage Site. Many of Sri Lanka's indigenous bird and mammal species can be found there. This Forest Reserve is home to more than 50% of Sri Lanka's indigenous mammals and almost 95% of its endemic birds.

The forest is always shrouded in a thick layer of rain clouds. It is a well-known destination in Galle. You may take in the splendour of vast, lush, and abundant evergreen rainforests as well as the tranquil pace of wildlife.

1160 LKR
